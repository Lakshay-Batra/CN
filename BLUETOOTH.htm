 Bluetooth

 

 Bluetooth is a wireless technology that will be used to provide personal area networks (PAN’s) or wireless wire connectivity between consumer and electronics devices.

 

(i)              The blue tooth was formed in 1998. The Bluetooth SIG (Special Interest Group) is a group of companies, led by telecommunication & computing industry leaders.

(ii)             It facilitates voice as well as data transmission.

(iii)            It supports one to one (point to point) or one to many (broad cast) communication.

(iv)           It is a set of ratio wave (2.4GHZ) communication protocols & standards that enable low coast high speed communication among device that is 10 m range of each other

 

 

1. Why is it called Bluetooth?

Sol:- King Harald Blatan (Bluetooth) of United Denmark & Norway in the 10th century.

  

2. What is blue tooth used for?

Sol:- It is used for: -

 

 (i)   Phone & Pagers.

 (ii)  Modems

 (iii) LAN access device

 (iv) Headsets

 (v)  Desktop & Handheld computer.

  

 

Bluetooth Terminology:-

 

1.   Piconet: - Several nodes are connected in a point to multipoint system. In piconet uses the master unit & slave unit. Any two Bluetooth devices that come within range of each other set up a connection  & thus forming a piconet. Up to 8 units (active) can form a piconet

2.  Master unit: The master in piconet whose clock & hooping sequence synchronizes other. Any unit can be master as there is no difference in H/W or S/W of the master & slave

3.  Slave unit: Devices in the piconet that are not the master.

4.  Scatternet: - Several piconet can exit in the same area are called scatternet.

5.  Mac address: Three-bit address that distinguishes each unit in a piconet.

6.  Parked units: Piconet devices that are synchronized but don’t have Mac address.

7.  Sniff, Hold & park mode: Power saving mode of a piconet device.

 

 

Host controller interface (HCI):-

 

   · Provides a uniform interface method for accessing the Bluetooth h/w capabilities.

   · Contains a command interface to the baseband controller, link manager & access to h/w status.

 

Link manager protocol (LMP):-

 

    · Responsible for link setup b/w Bluetooth units.

    · Handles the management of power modes, power consumption.

 

 

Service discovery protocol (SDP):-

 

    · Defines how a Bluetooth client’s application shall act to discover available Bluetooth server services & their characteristics.

    · Defines how a client can search for a service, based on specific attributes.

    · It provides means for discovery of new services becoming available when the client enters an area where a blue tooth server is operating.

    · Provides functionality for detecting when a service is no longer available.

 

 

OBEX Protocol (Object Exchange protocol):

 

     ·  In OBEX it is an optional application layer protocol.

     ·  It enables units sporting infra red communication to exchange a wide Varity of data & commands in a resource sensitive standardized fashion.

     ·  It used for client server model & is independent of transport mechanism.

     ·  RFCOMM is used as the main transport layer for OBEX.

 

  

Security: - Blue tooth provide security

 

        (i) Authentication:  –It is the processes of verify who is at the other hand of the link. It is performed for devices. It is used to prevent unwanted access to data.

        (ii) It is the process to check whether a device is permitted to access a service.

 

Advantage: -

 

      · It requires very less hardware. A compact chip-set.

      · Low price on blue tooth component.

      · Very low power consumption for blue tooth connection.

      · Diversity in product offering.
