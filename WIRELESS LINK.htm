Second Generation Mobile Systems

GSM (Global System for Mobile Communications)

GSM (Global System for Mobile communication) is a digital mobile telephone system using variation of TDMA (Time Division Multiple Access).

  • GSM World: Home of the GSM Association

  • Overview of the Global System for Mobile Communications: Excellent overview, background, and history

  • GSM Data Knowledge: Background on basic GSM terms and information and Intel's vision of the future of mobile data computing (including GPRS)

  • GSM Evolution: Discusses the basics of the GSM system and the future data transmission enhancements

  • mobileGPRS: All about General Packet Radio Service (GPRS) on mobile networks

IS-95 CDMA (Code Division Multiple Access)

  • CDMA Development Group: News, terminology, CDMA Data, Third Generation (3G), and Wireless Local Loop (WLL) discussions

  • CDMA vs. TDMA: High-level discussion of the merits of IS-95 vs. IS-54/D-AMPS

  • About CDMA: Qualcomm's overview of CDMA technology

D-AMPS/NADC TDMA (IS-54 & IS-136 & TIA/EIA-136)

D-AMPS (Digital-American Mobile Phone Service) was first specified as a standard in EIA/TIA Interim Standard 54 (IS-54). IS-136, is an evolved version of IS-54.

  • Ericsson's D-AMPS 1900 Overview: Discusses dual-mode dual-band concepts (sometimes referred to as PCS), and a comparison of D-AMPS and IS-95 CDMA

  • TDMA Tutorial: Overview of TDMA, migration, and future evolution

  • PDC (Personal Digital Cellular) & PHS (Personal Handyphone System)

  • Personal Digital Cellular (PDC) is based on Time Division Multiple Access,

  • PHS MoU Group: The complete source on Personal Handyphone System in Japan

  • DDI PHS Overview: Explains concepts and internet access scheme

  • PDC Overview: Ericsson's brief explanation of the PDC system

Third Generation Mobile Systems

  • The development of Third Generation (3G) Mobile systems, now collectively referred to as IMT-2000, but previously known as Future Public Land Mobile Telecommunications Service (FPLMTS) or Universal Mobile Telecommunications Services (UMTS).

  • IMT-2000: IMT-2000 official site

  • 3GPP: Third Generation Partnership Project 1- Global specifications for GSM/MAP network evolution to 3G and the UTRA RTT

  • 3GPP2: Third Generation Partnership Project 2- Global specifications for ANSI/TIA/EIA-41 network evolution to 3G and global specifications for the RTTs supported by ANSI/TIA/EIA-41

  • TDMA-EDGE & WIN Classroom: Tutorials, papers, slideshows, & presentations on EDGE Classic, EDGE Compact, TDMA, UWC-136, and more

  • The 3G Portal: Comprehensive new site with news, discussion groups, and more

  • 3G Pavilion: 3G Work of the CDMA Development Group. Includes cdma2000 information and slideshow

  • WCDMA: Ericsson's WCDMA Overview

  • 3G Newsroom: News, examples, reports, and more

Wireless Application Protocol (WAP)

The Wireless Application Protocol (WAP) is the de-facto world standard for wireless information and telephony services on digital mobile phones and other wireless terminals.

  • WAP Forum : WAP Forum is the organization controlling standardization of WAP.

  • WAP Developers Forum: Nokia's WAP Developers Forum

  • MobileInternet: Ericsson's free public domain WapIDE Service Development Kit with Application Designer, sample WAP browser, and more.

  • WAP.com: News, tools, and reviews

  • WAP.net: News, Developers Info, and More. Includes a WAP Developers Discussion Board on general WAP issues and a WAP SDK

  • HTML-Kit: HTML-Kit is a full-featured freeware text editor designed to help HTML, XML and script authors to edit, format, lookup help, validate, preview and publish web pages.

  • WapNuri DissectImage: A freeware graphics tool for creating WBMP format files.
